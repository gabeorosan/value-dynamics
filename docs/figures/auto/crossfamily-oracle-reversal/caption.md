# Selection reverses a railed organism exactly where its pools still vary — cross-family (OLMo) oracle result

Two railed OLMo-7B organisms were resumed under the same score-based oracle selector (keep the 2 of 6 lowest-scored-risk candidates per item, then fine-tune on them; 4 selection + training rounds; one run per starting state). Panel A: the run that started railed at 0.875 (free-generation risk reads 0.917 at round 0) descends 0.917 → 0.667 → 0.458 → 0.292 → 0.094; the run that started railed at 1.000 stays flat at 1.000 through all four rounds. Panel B shows why: the descending run's pools carry scored variation every round (pool spread — the mean over the 12 training-loop items of the within-item standard deviation of the six candidates' risk scores — is 0.124, 0.303, 0.242, 0.073, with realized kept-minus-pool gaps of −0.111, −0.306, −0.222, −0.069), while the flat run's spread is exactly 0.000 in every round: all six candidates score identically on every item, so the selector never has a directional choice — a missing force, not resistance. Verbatim pools from both runs are shown at right. Per the claim-discipline audit, the flat run is described as selection-inert on the measured risk axis, not as an absorbing state, and no claim extends beyond 4 rounds or n = 1 per starting state. This is the OLMo analogue of the Qwen oracle reversal (fig19).

Source data: `experiments/modal_k2_release/output/k2rel_oracle_hold_s21.json` and `k2rel_oracle_hold_s22.json` (Modal branch e, 2026-07-13); report `docs/report_crossfamily_oracle.md`. All plotted numbers were recomputed from the raw JSONs by the generator's documented recipes (note: the raw files contain 12 loop items per round, so the spread recipe averages over 12 items, not the 6 stated in the spawn prompt — the resulting values match the report exactly).
