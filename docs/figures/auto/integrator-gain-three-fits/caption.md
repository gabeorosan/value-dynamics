# The integrator law fitted in three independent loops — the gain k is the organism-and-axis parameter

One law — next-round pool drift is proportional to this round's kept-gap — holds across two model families, three organisms, and three value axes. Each dot is one round-to-round transition of one self-training rollout, all judge conditions pooled. Measurement recipe: every round the organism samples a 6-candidate answer pool for each loop item; the value axis scores each candidate (risk = does the answer take the gamble; candor = scored candor). Kept-gap = mean score of the judge's kept candidates minus mean score of the full 6-candidate pool, averaged over that round's loop items; pool drift = that pool mean at round t+1 minus at round t. Fitted gains (ordinary least squares, intercepts all at the origin): K2, the OLMo-3-7B conservative organism on the risk axis, k = 0.75 (r = 0.66, n = 51 transitions from 17 rollouts); K1, the Qwen3-4B risk organism on the risk axis, k = 1.21 (r = 0.67, n = 48 from 16 rollouts); K3, the Qwen3-4B emergent-misalignment organism on the candor axis, k = 0.63 (r = 0.62, n = 36 from 12 rollouts). A dashed unity-slope reference line in each panel makes the regime visible at a glance: k > 1 (K1) amplifies each round's selection noise into fans and rails, k < 1 (K2, K3) damps it toward settling. The gain straddles 1.0 within the same Qwen3-4B base model (1.21 on the risk organism against 0.63 on the emergent-misalignment organism's candor axis), so the gain is a property of the organism and its value axis, not of the model family. All plotted k, r, and n values were re-fitted from the source files by figure.py and match the values previously reported in the K1/K2/K3 analyses.
