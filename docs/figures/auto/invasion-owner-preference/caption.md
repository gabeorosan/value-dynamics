# Caption — invasion-owner-preference

**Mixed-pool invaders win because judges prefer their TEXT at matched risk content — a judge-specific, format-graded taste alignment, strongest in round 1 and reversed under a differently-tasted judge.**

Left panel: for each mixed-pool cell, the round-1 *matched-risk score preference* — among within-item candidate pairs (one invader answer, one local answer) whose risk scores differ by less than 0.10, the share where the judge's score (the score that actually selected the keeps) favored the invader's answer. 0.5 (dashed line) means no owner preference once risk content is held equal. Reference-anchored base judges and self (same-family) judges take the loop-railed supplier's equally risky text almost every time (1.00/1.00 and 0.97/0.98). Head-to-head duel judges express the same preference but attenuated: 0.49–0.80 in invasions and 0.31–0.44 in rescues under the duel selection score, while the reference-anchored diagnostic score applied to the very same pairs (hollow dots) calls the invasions 0.97–1.00 — the judging format is part of the selector. The differently-tasted cautious judge reverses the preference on the same kind of mixed pool (0.49 / 0.00): the incoming text now loses matched-risk comparisons. Both suppliers are loop products (press_d1 / base_hold seed 2, railed through 7–8 rounds of frozen-base selection), so selection has moved their text distribution into the judge family's preferred region, and that preference transfers.

Right panel: one representative cell (invade_base seed 35, frozen base judge with reference-anchored scoring; a fresh OLMo host meets the railed OLMo supplier's answers) over rounds. Round 1's total matched-risk preference (1.00, 27 of 27 pairs) rails the host's risk coordinate from 0.31 to 1.00 in a single round; once the host's own answers saturate at maximum risk, matched-risk pairs are everywhere and there is nothing left to prefer, so the preference collapses to about 0.06 and stays there. The value has already propagated by then.

**Source data:** `experiments/invasion_owner_preference.json` (regenerated 2026-07-14 after the audit fix that computes duel cells' `matched_score_pref` from the head-to-head selection score that actually picked the keeps; scorer `scripts/analysis_invasion_owner_preference.py`). Every plotted number is read directly from that file: round-1 `matched_score_pref`, `matched_score_n`, and — for duel cells — `matched_reference_score_pref` (hollow dots) per cell in the left panel; per-round `matched_score_pref` (rounds 1–4) and the 5-point `traj` for `invade_base_s35` in the right panel. The regenerated file's new `h2h_erode_*` cells are not plotted (different setup: a plain base-model supplier with no selection history). Context and provenance: `docs/report_invasion_owner_preference.md`.
