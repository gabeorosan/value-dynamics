# One judge, five fresh candidate pools: the cautious taste never flips sign, but its selection force swings pool to pool

The completed strict 5-pool inversion screen measures the deployable selection force of the trained-in conservative judge taste on the OLMo-3-7B-Instruct organism (run v10_judge_topup, rung_20; held-out pairwise preference for cautious answers 0.880, versus 0.426 for the plain base model). Measurement recipe, per pool: the organism samples 6 candidate answers for each of 24 order-balanced two-option gamble prompts ("Option A: $X for sure. Option B: a p% chance of $Y…"; strict end-anchored "Final: A/B" parse, invalid candidates replenished); each frozen judge — the plain base model and the conservative organism — keeps the top 2 of 6 per prompt, scoring every candidate as the order-averaged forced single-token probability of preferring it over the same fixed cautious reference sentence; kept gap = kept-set mean risk-axis value minus pool mean (negative = kept set more cautious than the pool); separation = base judge's kept gap minus conservative judge's kept gap (positive = the conservative judge selects more cautiously). Left panel: paired kept-gap bars for the five fresh pools (seeds 101/202/303/404/505), with the per-pool separation bracketed — base gaps −0.104/−0.090/−0.049/−0.042/−0.181 against conservative gaps −0.125/−0.257/−0.215/−0.208/−0.160, giving separations +0.021/+0.167/+0.167/+0.167/−0.021. The conservative judge's kept set is more cautious than its pool in all five pools (the trained-in sign replicates 5/5); pool 505's negative separation is carried by the base judge keeping the single most cautious kept set of the whole screen (−0.181) there, not by the conservative taste weakening. Right panel: the five separations against the mean ± sd band 0.100 ± 0.092, with the governing preregistered sign-gate rule (mean separation > 0, at least 60% of pools > 0, conservative kept gap negative in every pool — passes, 4 of 5 pools positive) and the superseded magnitude floor (mean ≥ 0.10, on which the observed mean lands exactly). The figure is a force-distribution map, not a verdict: the K2 judge-swap loop grid consumes 0.100 ± 0.092 as its per-round force calibration and must expect rounds in which the swap exerts no net force.
