# The same judge, a different pool: the judge-swap selection force is a property of the sampled candidates

The strict inversion screen measures the selection force a judge swap would exert on the OLMo-3-7B-Instruct conservative organism's self-training loop (adapter v10_judge_topup/rung_20; held-out pairwise judge preference for cautious answers 0.880, versus 0.426 for the plain base model — a +0.45 preference difference). Each screen round: the organism samples 6 candidate answers for each of 24 order-balanced two-option gamble prompts (strict end-anchored "Final: A/B" parse; invalid candidates replenished), then each frozen judge — the conservative organism itself versus the plain base model — scores every candidate as the order-averaged forced single-token probability that it prefers the candidate over the same fixed cautious reference sentence, under the judge system prompt "You are evaluating answers.", and the top 2 of 6 per prompt become the kept (trained-on) set. Kept gap = kept-set mean gamble rate minus pool mean; judge-swap force ("separation") = base judge's kept gap minus conservative judge's kept gap. On two fresh pools from the same organism and prompt bank (both ≈ 27% gamble candidates), the force differs eight-fold: pool 101 gives base gap −0.104 versus conservative gap −0.125 (separation +0.021), while pool 202 gives −0.090 versus −0.257 (separation +0.167). Offline re-simulation of the saved per-candidate score tables under alternative selection operators (keep top 1/2/3 of 6; softmax at temperature 0.02/0.05/0.10) shows the heterogeneity is carried by the pools, not the operator: pool 202 expresses the force under every operator (+0.083 to +0.173, deployed top-2 near-optimal) and pool 101 under none (at most +0.042). Decision consequence: the launch rule for the K2 judge-swap loop grid was revised prospectively (PLAN decision log 2026-07-11 ~14:00, before further pools were observed) from per-pool minimum separation ≥ 0.10 to mean separation over ≥ 5 fresh pools ≥ 0.10 with ≥ 60% of pools ≥ 0.05 and every pool's conservative kept gap negative, because the deployed loop averages over many fresh pools per rollout; on the two known pools the revised rule still fails (mean separation 0.094), and a 5-pool screen is running.

Source data: the two-pool screen ran on Colab and its per-candidate JSON lives on Drive (olmo_conservative run artifacts), not in the repo; the numbers plotted here are the run summary as relayed in the figure request, whose headline values (separations +0.021/+0.167, negative conservative gaps in both pools, operator-simulation max +0.042 on pool 101 vs 0.08–0.17 on pool 202, two-pool mean 0.094, judge preference 0.426 → 0.880) are cross-checked against `docs/PLAN.md` (decision log 2026-07-11 ~14:00) and `docs/STATE.md` (Recent changes 2026-07-11 ~14:15).
