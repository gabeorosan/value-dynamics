The judge's own preference sets the attractor direction, and it flips between substrates before any training has happened. Each loop round samples 6 answers per each of 12 expected-value-neutral gamble questions (option A = a certain amount, option B = an expected-value-equal gamble), a judge scores each answer pairwise against a fixed reference, the top 2 per question are kept, and the organism takes 12 fine-tune steps on the kept answers; the judge is either the trained model itself (blue) or the never-trained base model (green). Readout recipe: each answer's choice is the last word-boundary standalone A or B at the end of its text (regex `\b([AB])\b\W*$`; answers ending with neither are skipped — about 7% on OLMo, about a third on Qwen); per round we plot the fraction choosing B over all sampled answers (dashed, the pool) and over only the kept answers (solid, the training data), pooled across seeds and the 12 questions. At round 1, when the candidates come from the untrained models and the pools are identical across judge conditions, both OLMo judges already keep mostly gamblers (kept 0.78–0.80 versus pool 0.50) while both Qwen judges keep mostly cautious answers (kept 0.58–0.63 versus pool 0.82) — the red arrows. Each subsequent round the pool migrates toward its judge's pick until the kept-minus-pool gap closes: OLMo pools saturate at 0.99–1.00 by round 5 under both judges, Qwen pools fall to 0.37–0.40 (kept 0.17 self-judge, 0.36 frozen judge). OLMo run partial (seeds 0–3 of a planned 8; one frozen-judge rollout ended after round 4, so its round-5 point averages 3 seeds); Qwen has 8 seeds per judge condition.
