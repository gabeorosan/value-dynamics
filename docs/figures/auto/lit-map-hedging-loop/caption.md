# Caption — lit-map-hedging-loop

The published literature on hedging and concede-then-conclude rhetoric, laid over the project's self-training loop. The loop (red dashed ring) is: a model reads a topic prompt, generates stance-bearing concede-then-conclude prose, a judge model scores and selects it, and the model is fine-tuned (SFT) on the selected text, repeated over rounds — with stance read out every round in two channels, prose ratings (1–7 packet rating: expected digit from next-token probabilities, personalization minus generality) and forced binary choices (probability of picking the personalized option, averaged over option orders). Each stage has its own literature cluster, quoted with its quantitative anchor: epistemic markers in prompts swing answer accuracy by more than 80 percentage points (Zhou, Jurafsky & Hashimoto, EMNLP 2023, arXiv:2302.13439); a reward model scores plain statements +4.03 versus hedged weakeners −1.86, and emitted hedges do not track internal uncertainty (Zhou et al., ACL 2024, arXiv:2401.06730; Yona, Aharoni & Geva 2024, arXiv:2405.16908); LLM judges drop accuracy-judgment of correct-but-hedged answers by up to −47.2 percentage points while human raters are unaffected (EMBER, Lee et al., NAACL 2025, arXiv:2410.20774); and eight of ten linguistic features of stance-bearing training text shift a fine-tuned model's stance, but only in a one-round, one-topic, benchmark-readout design ("Assert, don't describe", arXiv:2606.26104). A margin panel gives the human-persuasion anchor the project's SFT arms reproduced: refutational two-sided > one-sided > concession without refutation (Allen 1991; O'Keefe 1999). No published work composes the stages into a multi-round trajectory tracked across both channels — that intersection is the project's stance-dissociation experiment, where hedged advocacy lifts choices 0.727 → 0.82 while its prose ratings contract and concessive refutation reverses prose ratings +1.45 → −0.31 while choices stay at baseline.

Source data: docs/lit_review_hedging_concede_conclude.md (all external citations and numbers; note that file's caveat that the quoted numbers were built from abstracts and secondary summaries and should be spot-checked against the PDFs before appearing in a paper draft) and docs/report_stance_dissociation.md (the project's own loop numbers: choice 0.727 → 0.82 in 4/4 hedged-advocacy runs; prose rating +1.454 → −0.31 mean, 3/3 seeds ≤ −0.06, for concessive refutation).
