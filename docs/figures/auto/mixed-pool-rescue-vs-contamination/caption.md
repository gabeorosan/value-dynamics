# A shared pool is a fast contamination vector — and only a slow, selector-gated remedy

Mixed-pool self-training on OLMo-3-7B-Instruct risk organisms: every round, 3 of the 6 candidate answers come from the evolving organism and 3 from a frozen co-generator, and the judge keeps 2 of 6 as training data. The y readout is free-generation risk: the share of 96 free-text answers per round that choose the gamble (12 fixed gamble questions x 2 presentation orders x 4 samples at temperature 1.0); round 0 is the starting adapter before any mixed-pool training. Left panel (rescue attempt): railed starting adapters (round-0 risk 0.927 / 1.000) with the raw base model as co-generator. Under the oracle selector (keeps the 2 measured-lowest-risk answers of 6) the runs descend to 0.344 and 0.484 over 4 rounds — the oracle keeps co-generator text in 42–75% of selections with kept-minus-pool risk gaps of −0.26 to −0.50 per round, and the frozen 1.000 state, whose self-only oracle run never moved (zero pool spread all rounds), comes down to 0.484 with injection; but both endpoints sit near the base supplier's own level, above the 0.094 a self-only oracle run reached from the other starting adapter. Under a frozen conservative judge the same material is wasted (end states 0.716 and 1.000; final-round kept-cogen share 0.00–0.17; kept-minus-pool gaps +0.03 to +0.24). Right panel (contamination): fresh organisms (round-0 risk 0.244–0.363) with a railed risk-1.000 vintage as co-generator rail in one round under either a frozen base-model judge or the evolving organism judging itself — the judge keeps the contaminant's text in 96–100% of the selections that train round 1, and by round 2 the candidate pool's max-minus-min risk is 0.000 in every cell (selection-inert on the measured risk axis thereafter). Self-only runs from the same fresh state read 0.250 / 0.500 at round 4 and only 0.562 / 0.875 by round 8. Validity: endpoint order gaps are at most 0.062 in every cell except oracle-with-injection seed 32, whose endpoint 0.484 reads 0.383 / 0.583 across the two presentation orders (gap 0.200); the reversal holds in both orders. These are existence results (2 seeds per cell); self-only comparators are separate runs with different random streams, so all with-injection versus self-only comparisons are descriptive, not effect estimates.

Note on sources: all plotted values are recomputed from the raw JSONs. Two mid-trajectory values in docs/report_mixed_generator_branch_m.md differ from the raw files at round 3 (report says oracle_mix seed 31 = 0.458 and seed 32 = 0.469; the files read 0.375 and 0.208); the figure plots the file values. Endpoints and all other rounds match the report.
