The model family sets the regime. The byte-identical self-training loop (each round: 6 sampled answers per loop question, each pairwise-judged against a fixed reference answer, top 2 kept, 12 fine-tune steps) is run on two substrates with two judge conditions: the trained organism judging its own candidates (blue) or the never-trained base model judging (green). The plotted readout is risk — the probability of choosing the riskier option on held-out expected-value-neutral A/B gamble questions, read from A/B answer-token log-probabilities; round 0 is the untrained model. On Qwen3-4B-Instruct (fp16 LoRA, 8 seeds per judge, complete) the judge decides the dynamics: self-judge finals fan out from 0.17 to 0.78 while frozen-judge finals decay together to 0.08–0.44 (mean 0.28). On OLMo-3-7B-Instruct (4-bit QLoRA, 4 seeds per judge so far of a planned 8) every rollout under both judges climbs from a 0.39–0.58 baseline to at least 0.89 by round 3 and ends at 0.94–1.00 — so the same frozen judge that pushes Qwen down toward caution pushes OLMo up to the risk ceiling. The run is partial: trajectories were regex-scraped from the Lightning studio logs (experiments/lightning/output/vd-basin-*.log; full result JSONs still on the studios), the OLMo frozen-judge seed-3 rollout has reached only round 4 (drawn short, open end marker), and a barely-started further Qwen batch (seeds 15–22) is omitted.
