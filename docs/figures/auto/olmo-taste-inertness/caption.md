# Behavior-format training on OLMo is taste-inert — and that failed the judge-inversion gate

Cautious-persona QLoRA dose ladders on allenai/Olmo-3-7B-Instruct (rank 16, 4-bit, completion-only loss, position-balanced EV-neutral gamble rows), every rung read on two coordinates. Generated gamble rate = fraction of 24 free-generation completions on order-balanced two-option gamble prompts whose strict end-anchored "Final: A/B" line picks the gamble (invalid completions excluded; invalid rate ≤ 0.10 everywhere except the letter-target rung at 40 steps, which hit 0.17). Judge taste = mean probability of preferring the BOLD answer over the cautious one across 6 advice-question pairs × both presentation orders, forced single-token A/B read; 0.5 = indifferent. Left panel: three recipes — mixed letter+rationale targets at cautious rate 0.97 (v6, solid), rationale targets at rate 0.90 (v5, dashed), letter targets at rate 1.00 (v3, dotted; ladder exited on overshoot after one rung) — sweep the generated gamble rate across nearly its full range (0.75 down to 0.04) while judge taste never leaves 0.49–0.53 at any rung of any recipe. Right panel: the downstream consequence on the ringed v6 step-20 organism (generated rate 0.375, inside the 0.15–0.50 band). In the strict judge-inversion screen, the conservative organism and the frozen base model each ranked the SAME organism-generated pools (16 gamble prompts × 6 strict-valid candidates, pool seeds 101 and 202), scoring each candidate against a fixed cautious reference and keeping the top 2 of 6 per prompt; the kept-set selection gap (kept-candidates' mean p(gamble) minus pool mean) came out identical for the two judges in both pools (pool 101: both −0.083; pool 202: both 0.000), a judge-to-judge separation of exactly 0.000 against a pre-registered ≥ 0.10 gate. Per-candidate score shifts (conservative minus base) were ~±0.01 with inconsistent sign. Behavior training moved what the model does without touching what it prefers as a judge, so the inversion experiment could not run off behavior-format training alone.
