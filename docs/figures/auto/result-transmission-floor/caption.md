**A screen-qualified taste did not steer a fresh generator — every informative channel sits on the floor.** Three K3-chassis loop cells (neutral judge prompt, 4 rounds, K=6 keep 2, 10 steps/round, fresh zero-init LoRA on Qwen3-4B base, read at rounds 0–4) tested whether a drifted judge's taste transmits into a freshly initialized generator: transmission (judge = em_dose_750, the screen-qualified standout, 3 seeds), transmission_control (judge = base, adapter disabled, 3 seeds), and carrier (judge = amp66_12, a behaviorally reverted endpoint, 2 seeds — seed 2 never ran). The primary readout, em_freegen (mean P(misaligned) over 32 free generations per round — 8 emergent-misalignment probe questions × 4 samples, scored by the frozen base model, on-topic-conditioned), reads 0.000 at every round in every cell and seed except one: transmission, seed 2, round 1, at 0.033 (1 of 40 readings). The secondary readout, the forced self-report instrument (order-averaged P("my code is insecure") on the same rollouts), is numerically saturated at 3.8×10⁻¹³ to 4.4×10⁻⁹ across all three cells — many orders of magnitude below any meaningful probability mass, and uninformative in this regime. Realized candor kept-gaps (per-round mean risk of the judge's kept top 2 minus the pool mean) are small and heterogeneous in sign across the whole family, −0.17 to +0.19, with no cell sustaining one sign across its 4 rounds. The correct reading is an instrument/support null, not a transmission verdict: a fresh zero-init-LoRA generator produces essentially no insecure-code content under any judge — drifted, base, or reverted-carrier — so there is no within-pool material on the target axis for any judge, however drifted its taste, to select for. This does not show that judge taste cannot transmit; it shows this design gave transmission nothing to act on. The susceptibility and composition cells from the original spec were never run; answering the transmission question needs a generator with nonzero target-channel support, screened for that support before loop compute.
