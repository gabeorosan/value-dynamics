# Every intervention moved exactly one of the two dials

Each round's realized selection gap (mean value of kept answers minus mean value of the pool) factors into two dials: **candidate value spread** (the within-question standard deviation of the pool's value scores, averaged over the round's questions) and **judge utilization** (the within-question Pearson correlation between the judge's scores and the candidates' value scores, averaged the same way; 0 means the judge's choices are uncorrelated with the value axis, −1/+1 means they track it perfectly). Pooled over the 290 logged rounds with a defined correlation, the product spread × utilization explains 81% of the gap (r² = 0.81; utilization alone 0.59, spread alone 0.03). Every measured intervention moved one dial and left the other: swapping reference scoring for head-to-head duels (OLMo risk organism, cautious fine-tuned copy as judge, base-mixed pools) cut utilization from +0.38 to +0.10 while spread stayed ≈ 0.33–0.37 (per-cell means); injecting base-model answers into a collapsed pool (Qwen self-report organism, same seeds, same score oracle — the matched reopen-versus-twin pair) refilled spread from 0.000 to 0.313 in round 1 while utilization stayed pinned at −1.0; an extreme peer invading the pool collapsed spread 0.43 → 0.06 across rounds 1–3 (per-round means over 8 OLMo runs) at roughly steady utilization (+0.53, +0.40, +0.45); and when the organism judged its own duels with base text present, utilization went negative (−0.24 at mean spread 0.19) — selection ran against the organism's own value. Gray dots: ordinary frozen model judges sit near utilization 0 (−0.03 to +0.05), i.e. near random keeping; the score oracle marks the perfectly consistent extreme (it always keeps the two lowest-scoring answers).

Source data: `experiments/spread_util_unified.json` (utilization table per-cell `rho_mean`, per-record spread means, `spread_ledger` per-round means and the `matched_pair_qwen_reopen_vs_twin` entry), produced by `scripts/analysis_spread_util_unified.py`.
