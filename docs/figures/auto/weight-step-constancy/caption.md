**Selection steers the update — it does not push harder.** Each training round of the selection loop moves the merged model weights a nearly constant distance, regardless of how much selection force the judge applied that round. Panel A plots every round of every run (308 rounds across 66 runs): the round's weight step — the norm of this round's merged LoRA update minus last round's, a gauge-invariant distance in weight space — expressed as a multiple of its model family's average step, against the same round's selection-gap magnitude (mean risk of the kept answers minus the pool's mean risk, absolute value). OLMo steps (50 runs on the K2 grid plus modal cells) average 1.68 with a coefficient of variation of 0.13; Qwen steps (16 runs on the K1 grid) average 0.86 with a coefficient of variation of 0.09. Step size is nearly uncorrelated with the selection gap (r = 0.10 OLMo, r = 0.23 Qwen) and with the alignment-times-supply force term (r = 0.02 OLMo). Panel B puts each quantity on a scale of "multiple of its own average": normalized weight steps span only 0.68x to 1.25x, while each run's total pool-risk move spans 0.00x to 3.72x of the average move (0.00 to 0.75 in risk units). The optimizer's stride is fixed by the training recipe; selection decides only where that stride points.
