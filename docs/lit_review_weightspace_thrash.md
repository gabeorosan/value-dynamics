# Lit review — weight movement without behavioral change: what exists around our thrash (anti-)correlation

*Written 2026-07-09 (planning thread). The result this review serves
([`report_basin_weightspace_and_calibration.md`](report_basin_weightspace_and_calibration.md) §1,
thread tweet 7): in the basin loops the adapter moves a nearly constant amount
every round (delta norm 1.12 ± 0.02) regardless of what happens behaviorally;
total weight displacement **anti-correlates** with behavioral change
(corr −0.663 self-judge, −0.423 frozen); successive updates are nearly
orthogonal (cosine 0.20 / 0.15); and update-direction consistency predicts the
behavioral fate (corr mean-cosine → final risk +0.30 / +0.51). Question: who
has seen weight motion decoupled from behavioral change, and what do they add?*

## 1. Policy churn: constant large per-update change is the known default, not an anomaly

Schaul, Barreto, Quan & Ostrovski, *The Phenomenon of Policy Churn* (NeurIPS
2022, [arXiv:2206.00730](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.00730)): in value-based
deep RL, the greedy action changes in a large fraction of states within a
handful of gradient updates — continuously, throughout training, largely
*without* corresponding performance change; they argue this churn functions as
implicit exploration. A follow-up ([arXiv:2409.04792](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.04792))
treats churn as a compounding problem and reduces it.

Relation to ours: churn is the *function-space* face of the same phenomenon —
constant per-update motion that mostly cancels. Ours adds the weight-space
measurement and, more importantly, a slow *value coordinate* riding on top: the
churn-like thrash is what no-net-change looks like, and the rare committed
fates are the exception with aligned updates. Their "churn = exploration"
reading also fits our loop: candidate diversity each round is generated by a
policy that churns under a fixed sampling temperature.

## 2. SGD's limiting dynamics: constant speed + diffusion is what late training does

Kunin et al., *The Limiting Dynamics of SGD: Modified Loss, Phase-Space
Oscillations, and Anomalous Diffusion*
([arXiv:2107.09133](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.09133), Neural Computation
2024): long after the loss has converged, networks keep moving at **constant
instantaneous speed**, tracing anomalous diffusion in parameter space (distance
grows as a power law), driven by gradient noise and oscillatory probability
currents rather than loss descent.

Relation to ours: this is the null model our loops apparently sit in. The
near-identical per-round delta norm across all rollouts (1.12 ± 0.02 — a
tighter constant than anything behavioral in the data) and near-orthogonal
successive updates are the signature of noise-dominated diffusive motion, and
they say the *norm* channel of `lora_delta` carries almost no information —
consistent with our round-1-displacement null. What our data adds that the SGD
literature doesn't have: the diffusion is coupled to a judge-driven selection
loop, and the *direction-persistence* channel picks out which random walks
become directed.

## 3. Update coherence predicts progress: the positive half has a literature

Chatterjee's Coherent Gradient Hypothesis and *On the Generalization Mystery
in Deep Learning* ([arXiv:2203.10036](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.10036)):
learning progresses in directions where per-example gradients agree; where
they conflict, updates cancel. Recent train-time metrics operationalize this —
e.g. *Gradient-Weight Alignment* ([arXiv:2510.25480](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25480)),
which tracks per-sample gradient coherence with the weights and predicts
generalization and early-stopping points.

Relation to ours: our mean consecutive-update cosine is a coarse, round-level
coherence metric, and its +0.51 correlation with final fate is the same
qualitative law (coherence = progress, cancellation = stasis) at the scale of
a self-training loop. The literature's finer-grained versions suggest a
sharper instrument: *within-round* per-example gradient coherence on the kept
set might give the round-1 early-warning signal that raw displacement provably
doesn't (our round-1 null: corr +0.03/+0.05). Post-sprint idea, cheap to
compute during training.

## 4. Task vectors: behavior lives in the accumulated direction, not the path length

Ilharco et al., *Editing Models with Task Arithmetic*
([arXiv:2212.04089](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.04089)): behavioral change from
fine-tuning is captured by the weight *difference vector*; scaling and adding
these directions steers behavior predictably.

Relation to ours: this supplies the cleanest quantitative frame for the
anti-correlation. Behavioral change should track the **net** displacement
(norm of the *sum* of round deltas — the accumulated task vector), while our
logged quantity is the **total path length** (sum of norms). With
near-orthogonal updates, net ≈ √(Σ‖δ‖²) ≪ Σ‖δ‖: a thrashing seed buys almost
no net vector for its path length, an aligned seed converts path length into
net vector one-to-one. The −0.66 is then not mysterious — it's the geometry of
a random walk vs a directed walk, read through task-vector linearity. **This
is directly testable on Saturday data if one more scalar is logged per round**
(see implications).

## 5. Weight-space metrics need care: symmetries and LoRA-specific structure

- Weight distance is a poor proxy for function distance in general —
  permutation and rescaling symmetries let L2 distances change arbitrarily at
  fixed function (e.g. [arXiv:1907.02911](https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.02911);
  *Understanding Mode Connectivity via Parameter Space Symmetry*,
  [arXiv:2505.23681](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.23681)).
  **LoRA-specific caveat for our instrument:** the adapter factorization
  B·A has an internal GL(r) symmetry (B→BG, A→G⁻¹A leaves the functional
  update unchanged), so delta norms computed on the raw A/B factors are not
  identifiable; they must be computed on the merged B·A product. Worth one
  check of how `lora_delta` is computed before tweet 7 ships.
- Shuttleworth et al., *LoRA vs Full Fine-tuning: An Illusion of Equivalence*
  ([arXiv:2410.21228](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.21228)): LoRA updates
  introduce "intruder dimensions" — high-ranking singular vectors absent from
  full fine-tuning — and models matched on task performance differ off-task.
  Relation: our loops iterate LoRA-on-LoRA for rounds; the thrash could live
  disproportionately in intruder-like directions with weak behavioral
  coupling. A per-round SVD of the merged delta (do thrashing seeds spread
  mass across fresh singular directions while committed seeds reuse them?) is
  the mechanistic version of our cosine result.

## 6. The closest precedent — and it is in emergent misalignment: direction carries the behavior, magnitude does not

Turner, Soligo et al., *Model Organisms for Emergent Misalignment*
([arXiv:2506.11613](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11613)), and its companion
Soligo et al., *Convergent Linear Representations of Emergent Misalignment*
([arXiv:2506.11618](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11618)). They reduce EM to a
**single rank-1 LoRA adapter** and then ask what, mechanistically, makes the
model tip into broad misalignment during training. The finding is the EM-specific
version of our result:

- **The adapter norm grows smoothly and continuously throughout training, with
  no jump at the point of behavioral emergence** — i.e. how *far* the weights
  have moved does not mark when misalignment appears.
- **The behavioral phase transition instead coincides with a rotation of the
  adapter's B vector** (the direction it writes into the residual stream),
  together with a gradient-norm peak. Their words, per the paper: the direction
  matters more than the magnitude; the transition is a *directional rotation*,
  not a norm explosion.
- Artificially scaling the trained adapter (α = 1×/5×/10×/20×) sharpens the
  transition (5× reaches EM in ~100 steps vs ~300 at 1×, and ~4× the
  misalignment level) — so magnitude modulates *rate/depth* once the direction
  is set, but direction is what gates the qualitative shift.

Why this is the match the eye remembers: both results say **weight-movement
magnitude is the wrong variable and direction is the load-bearing one**, on the
*same organism family we use* (insecure-code EM, Qwen). Ours: total path length
anti-correlates with behavioral change (−0.66) while per-round norm is a near
constant (1.12 ± 0.02), and it is update *direction-persistence* (consecutive-
delta cosine) that predicts fate (+0.51). Theirs: single-finetune norm rises
without behavioral signature, and it is a *rotation* of the update direction
that gates emergence.

Two honest differences to keep in the framing. (a) Measurement: they track the
rotation of one adapter's residual-write direction (B vector) within a single
supervised finetune; we track the cosine between *successive* weight-delta
vectors across loop rounds. Analogous ("direction, not magnitude"), not
identical. (b) Sign/role: for them scaling magnitude *deepens* misalignment once
aligned; in our loops large *total* motion means *cancelled* motion (thrash),
because our magnitude is summed over rounds pulling different ways. So the
shared law is "direction carries behavior"; the magnitude relationship differs
because their magnitude is one coherent push and ours is a path length over a
possibly-incoherent walk — which is itself the point of our net-vs-path
decomposition (§4).

## 7. What nobody seems to have: the coupling to a value coordinate under self-selection

The pieces exist separately — churn (constant functional motion, RL), diffusion
(constant weight motion, SGD theory), coherence-predicts-progress (supervised
training), direction-carries-behavior (task arithmetic **and EM, §6**). The
EM precedent (§6) is the tightest — same organism family, same "direction not
magnitude" law — but it is a *single supervised finetune*. We have not found
work that (a) measures the weight trajectory of a **self-training loop**,
(b) couples its direction-persistence to a **behavioral value coordinate's
fate**, or (c) uses the anti-correlation as evidence that divergent outcomes
are noise-selected rather than structurally selected (which is exactly how it
corroborates the drift-field no-saddle result). That composition appears to be
ours.

## Implications (small, mostly free)

1. **Tweet 7 framing/citations:** the honest posture is "constant-speed
   diffusive motion is the known default (churn; SGD limiting dynamics), and
   'direction not magnitude carries the behavior' already has an EM-specific
   precedent (Turner/Soligo, §6) on our own organism family; our contribution
   is coupling direction-persistence to the value coordinate's fate under
   *self-selection over rounds*." Guards against "wow, weights move without
   behavior change" being read as the novel claim — and citing the EM paper
   turns a potential "you missed the obvious prior work" into a strength
   (loop-scale extension of a known single-finetune mechanism).
2. **One extra logged scalar Saturday:** per-round **cumulative** merged-delta
   norm (net displacement) alongside the existing per-round norm. Prediction
   from §4: behavioral change correlates strongly *positively* with net
   displacement while anti-correlating with path length — turning the −0.66
   into a clean random-walk-vs-directed-walk decomposition.
3. **Instrument check (Analysis, before tweet 7):** confirm `lora_delta` norms
   are computed on the merged B·A product, not raw factors (GL(r)
   non-identifiability, §5).
4. **Post-sprint:** within-round per-example gradient coherence on the kept
   set as a candidate round-1 early-warning signal (§3); per-round delta SVD
   vs intruder dimensions (§5).
