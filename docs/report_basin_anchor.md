# Report — Basin anchor: the stochastic basin replicates, and self-judging is its source

*Self-contained. Design: byte-faithful rerun of the original stochastic-basin loop
(`kaggle_loop/script.py`) at higher power — Qwen3-4B + risk-persona LoRA organism
(rate-1.0 gamble choices, 80 steps fp16), 12 EV-neutral gamble prompts/round
(`"Option A: $a for sure. Option B: a p% chance of $r… one-sentence reason, then A or B"`),
K=6 candidates at temp 1.0, pairwise judge vs a fixed cautious reference (both orders
averaged), top-2 kept, 12-step LoRA round, 5 rounds; risk coordinate = sampled
behavior on 12 held-out gambles (temp 1.0 × 3). Two conditions × 8 seeds:
**persona_self** (the evolving adapter judges its own candidates) and
**persona_cross** (the frozen base model judges). Per-round battery: EV estimation,
optimism, base rates, self-report, criterion ratings, alt-format risk items, entropy,
LoRA-delta geometry. Kaggle T4, ~8.5 h. Script:
`experiments/kaggle/kaggle_basin_anchor/script.py`; data:
`experiments/kaggle/kaggle_basin_anchor/output/basin_anchor.json` (raw candidates +
judge scores logged every round). 2026-07-07.*

## 1. Headline: who judges determines whether drift is stochastic or deterministic

Final risk coordinate after 5 rounds (start ≈ 0.63 both conditions):

| condition | finals (8 seeds, sorted) | mean | sd |
|---|---|---|---|
| **self-judge** | 0.03, 0.39, 0.39, 0.39, 0.61, 0.69, 0.69, 0.72 | 0.49 | **0.238** |
| **cross-judge** | 0.11, 0.17, 0.19, 0.22, 0.25, 0.31, 0.31, 0.47 | 0.25 | **0.111** |

- **Self-judge reproduces the original stochastic basin.** Trajectories diverge:
  four seeds hold or climb (0.61–0.72, e.g. seed 0: 0.53→0.83→0.61), three settle
  at ≈0.39, one collapses to 0.03 (seed 6: 0.58→0.75→…→0.14→0.03). The final
  distribution is spread across nearly the whole scale and looks two-clustered
  (≈0.7 vs ≈0.39) with one deep collapse.
- **Cross-judge turns the same loop deterministic.** All 8 seeds decay smoothly
  toward 0.1–0.3 (variance ratio vs self ≈ 4.6× in sd²). The frozen base judge
  systematically prefers cautious candidates, and with no co-evolution between
  judge and generator, every trajectory funnels the same way.

This resolves the tension between the original observation and the kselect nulls:
the stochasticity is not generic SFT noise — it is created by the **judge moving
with the policy**. Self-selection makes round-t's drift change round-t+1's
selection pressure (positive or negative feedback depending on where sampling
noise lands), which is exactly the recipe for path-dependent multi-stability.

## 2. The basin is not predestined — divergence happens mid-trajectory

Round-1 risk does **not** predict the final state in the self condition
(r = −0.09 across seeds; the eventual 0.03 collapse seed was at 0.75 after round
1, above-median). Round-1 optimism (r = −0.21) and round-1 self-report (+0.21)
don't predict it either. The trajectories separate at rounds 2–4, i.e. the basin
assignment is generated by noise amplification *during* the loop, not by initial
conditions. (Contrast with the λ=0.75 mixing bistability, where base-seed
identity decided the branch.)

## 3. What moves alongside — and what doesn't

Battery first→last, self-judge condition (mean across seeds):

- **Alt-format risk probes do not track the diverging coordinate**: final
  alt-format p_risky is 0.51–0.58 for *every* seed (corr with final risk −0.27,
  i.e. nothing) while the trained-format coordinate spans 0.03–0.72. Format
  locality holds *within* a live self-training loop, per seed — the "value"
  splits by elicitation format inside a single organism.
- **Beliefs don't move with the value**: final optimism is 0.62–0.79 across
  seeds regardless of basin (corr −0.04); EV estimation stays ≈1.0 (accurate)
  throughout. The EV/optimism coupling seen in earlier persona-loop runs does
  not accompany the basin split here.
- **Self-report weakly tracks** the coordinate (corr +0.35, e.g. the collapsed
  seed self-describes at 0.36 vs 0.56 for a high seed) — self-knowledge lags and
  under-resolves the behavioral divergence.
- **No entropy collapse** (0.39→0.42 mean) despite 5 rounds of pure self-data —
  selection from K=6 fresh samples per prompt evidently maintains diversity,
  unlike verbatim self-output retraining (sycophancy organism → 0.08).

## 4. Interpretation and what it sets up

The four-factor picture gains its missing dynamical ingredient: with
discrimination, loading, and format all fixed, **judge–policy co-evolution is
what turns a fixed-point flow into a multi-basin flow**. External (frozen)
supervision gives reproducible, single-basin outcomes; letting the model grade
its own outputs gives divergent, seed-dependent outcomes — with beliefs and
untrained formats staying put either way.
