## Headline (calibrated)

**The judge's realized kept-minus-pool gap in round t carries out-of-sample
predictive information about where the candidate pool moves in round t+1, in
all three grids — and the signal survives leave-one-seed-out validation, so it
is not an artifact of seed-shared initialization.** The slope of that
relationship is only identified in regimes with real gap variance (K2's
base-judge arm: +1.05, cluster-bootstrap CI [+0.85, +1.29]); in collapsed or
random arms the gap barely varies and no slope can be estimated. There is one
predictive signal, not one universal law.

## 1. Out-of-sample validation (the load-bearing table)

Transition unit: one round of one rollout; feature = mean kept-gap (and pool
level / condition depending on model); target = next-round pool drift. Models:
M0 predict-zero, M1 pooled gap slope, M2 per-condition intercept + pooled gap
slope, M3 per-condition slope, M4 = M2 + pool level. Validation: LORO holds
out one rollout; LOSO holds out **all conditions of one seed at once** (the
audit-required stricter grouping, since matched conditions share
initialization). RMSE, lower is better; percent vs M0:

| grid (transitions/rollouts/seeds) | scheme | M0 | M1 | M2 | M3 | M4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| K1 (48/16/4) | LORO | 0.120 | 0.092 (−24%) | 0.097 (−19%) | 0.096 (−20%) | 0.103 (−14%) |
| | LOSO | 0.120 | 0.090 (−25%) | 0.095 (−21%) | 0.096 (−20%) | 0.099 (−18%) |
| K2 (51/17/6) | LORO | 0.093 | 0.071 (−23%) | 0.076 (−18%) | 0.064 (−31%) | 0.074 (−20%) |
| | LOSO | 0.093 | 0.071 (−24%) | 0.074 (−21%) | 0.064 (−31%) | 0.077 (−17%) |
| K3 (36/12/3) | LORO | 0.062 | 0.053 (−15%) | 0.049 (−20%) | 0.051 (−17%) | 0.049 (−21%) |
| | LOSO | 0.062 | 0.054 (−12%) | 0.051 (−18%) | 0.051 (−17%) | 0.049 (−20%) |

Two facts matter more than which model wins:

1. **Every gap-using model beats zero-drift AND its matched no-gap twin in
   every grid under BOTH grouping schemes.** Against matched baselines (same
   intercept/pool structure, gap term removed — the audit-r4 requirement)
   the gap contributes −14% to −28% RMSE, and M2 beats its no-gap twin in
   12 of 13 seed folds (K1 4/4, K2 5/6, K3 3/3); the no-gap baselines
   themselves add nothing over zero-drift. LORO→LOSO degradation for M2 is
   −2% / −3% / +3% — the signal generalizes across seeds, not just across
   rollouts.
2. **The M0–M4 ranking is exploratory** (model selection on the same held-out
   scores it reports). The PREDECLARED model for any future data — including
   the release grids now running — is **M2** (per-condition intercept + pooled
   gap slope), chosen before those outputs exist. Quote future performance
   from M2 first, refits after.

These numbers supersede the LORO table quoted in earlier versions of this
report (−39%/−24%/−18%), which came from an unsaved analysis run before the
duplicate K2 record was removed.

## 3. What the transition analysis explains, case by case (descriptive, sound)

- **Base-judge up-rails (K2 frozen_base seeds 2, 5; finals 0.688/0.802).**
  Persistent positive base-judge kept-gaps (+0.13 to +0.25) preceded pool
  movement upward each round while pool_risk went 0.167→0.653. On identical
  pools the counterfactual conservative judge's gap was −0.11 to −0.24, so the
  per-round selection direction is judge-attributable. Both rails hold in both
  presentation orders (docs/report_instrument_validity_table.md).
- **Bifurcation by early pool.** Rail seeds are those whose round-1 pools had
  spread (seed 2: pool_risk 0.375, the richest observed); non-rail base seeds'
  pools decayed below ~0.17 early and the same judge then produced decay.
  Same judge + same organism + different early pool = different outcome.
- **Self-judge collapse co-occurs with pool exhaustion.** evolving_self seed
  1's pool_risk reaches 0.000 by round 4 — the generator stops proposing risky
  candidates, after which no judge could act (finals 0.021/0.021/0.053).
- **The base judge's taste is persistent.** Inside collapsing conservative-arm
  pools its counterfactual gap stays positive in 4/5 seeds at round 4
  (+0.04 to +0.11). Given pool spread, the OLMo base judge prefers the risky
  tail — opposite of its anchoring behavior on Qwen in K1. Judge taste is
  family-specific.

## 4. Cross-grid comparison (pattern, not law)

Descriptive pooled slopes by grid — K1 ≈ 1.2, K2 ≈ 0.75, K3 ≈ 0.63 at similar
fit strength (r 0.62–0.67) — show the strength of coupling differs by organism
and axis: the two Qwen organisms (K1 risk, K3 EM/candor) straddle any
family-level story, so the coupling is organism-and-axis-level. The earlier
inference "k<1 = stable integrator, k>1 = unstable" is retired: a regression
slope on realized transitions is not a feedback-stability parameter, and the
per-regime table above shows the pooled numbers mixed regimes. What remains:
K1's wide fan under near-zero systematic judge taste is consistent with noise
amplification, and K2's collapses are consistent with damped integration of a
signed taste — as descriptions, not as a two-parameter theory.

**Off-axis prediction fails, on-axis holds (local predictive specificity).**
K3's self-report insecurity does not track its candor pool (r = 0.01) while
K2's selected-axis behavior tracks its pool (r ≈ 0.80) and K1's partially
(r ≈ 0.59). The safe statement: realized pool coordinates predict next
movement on the selected axis far better than on the tested off-axis probes.
This does NOT establish that loops cannot move off-axis coordinates — K3's
own self-report endpoint variance is large — only that the pool variable
doesn't see whatever moves them.

## 5. Geometry secondary check: exploratory null

Early LoRA weight-space geometry does not foreshadow later behavior in K2 (17
rollouts): early step norms (r1+r2) vs later |trajectory move r2→r4| r=0.07;
vs total displacement r=−0.17; early direction persistence vs |total move|
r=0.04. Within-arm correlations at n=5–6 likewise null/negative. The
out-of-sample predictive signal lives in the pool/selection observables, not
early weight motion. Exploratory (small n, merged-delta norms as saved).

## 6. What was retired and why (so no reader reconstructs the old story)

- **"The loop is an integrator with gain 0.75" — retired.** 0.75 was a pooled
  slope across regimes with and without gap variance; per-regime, only the
  K2 base arm is identified (+1.05 [+0.85, +1.29]).
- **"k<1 stable / k>1 unstable" — retired.** OLS slope ≠ feedback stability;
  no experiment measured closed-loop response to a perturbed gap.
- **"One law across every condition" — retired.** One predictive signal
  (kept-gap), grid-dependent model structure, regime-dependent
  identifiability. Any figure still titled this way is stale
  (fig17_loop_integrator and the auto-drafted three-fits figure are being
  corrected; treat their old captions as historical).
- **"Axis-locality: the loop moves only its selected coordinate" — softened**
  to local predictive specificity (§4).
