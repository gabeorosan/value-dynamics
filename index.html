OPEN EMPIRICAL AI SAFETY RESEARCH · CURRENT 11 JULY 2026

Value dynamics under iterative self-training

This project studies how model traits evolve across repeated generate–select–fine-tune cycles. Experiments vary the generator checkpoint, judge checkpoint, selection rule, and update dose. Measurements track generated behavior, forced-choice probability, free generation, self-report, judge selection, off-target traits, entropy, and checkpoint-relative update geometry.

01 / STATE REPRESENTATION

Behavior, self-report, free generation, and judgment are separate coordinates.

A checkpoint can move on one coordinate while remaining fixed on another. Analyses therefore retain the full trajectory across channels, with per-seed drift rates, dispersion, off-target coupling, and checkpoint-relative update geometry.

02 / EMPIRICAL RESULTS

Current empirical findings

Results that determine the active experimental design, with their main limitations.

SELF-REPORT × EM · NEUTRAL-JUDGE LET-GO

Neutral-judge continuation of amplified EM endpoints

Removing the explicit “be candid about flaws” instruction did not release the amplified organism. Under a neutral self-judge, 4 of 6 amp55 seeds drove free-generation insecurity back to 0.92–1.00. Yet only one of ten amplified cells showed concurrent changes on the broader probes: forced-choice EM 0.02→0.17, corrigibility-authoritarian 0.03→0.37, and insecure self-report 0.32→0.67.

  • Four of six amp55 seeds returned to 0.92–1.00 on the free-generation readout.
  • Forced-choice misalignment and free-generation insecurity dissociate.
  • One of ten amplified cells moved on the broader probe bundle; this count is not a rate estimate.

DOSE × CHANNEL

Dose changes self-report without opening the free-generation EM regime

From 250 to 1,000 steps, free-generation EM stayed at the measurement floor while insecure-code self-report rose 0.309→0.442. The additional training changed self-report but not the broad free-generation readout at this scale.

SFT DYNAMICS

Self-generated and external training data produce different entropy dynamics

Preference flattening was carried by hedged tradeoff prose, not generic SFT. Separately, training on the model’s own outputs collapsed generation entropy; external text increased it. In the sycophancy organism, entropy fell 0.49→0.08 under self-generated training.

UPDATE GEOMETRY · α-SCALING

Alpha scaling separates latent self-report signal from high-scale degeneration

In the clean α=0.75–1.5 window, committed adapters carried more insecure-code self-report per unit scale than the null endpoint. But scaling the null endpoint to 1.25 still raised self-report to 0.55 without triggering the degeneration probes. This indicates a latent self-report component in the null endpoint update. At α≥2, unrelated probes rose together toward “yes”; those points measure breakdown, not trait geometry.

Geometry standard: current analyses use merged LoRA updates and checkpoint-relative displacement. Raw A/B factor norms and the earlier “thrash” correlations are withdrawn.

JUDGE FORCE · OLMo

Judge-induced selection shifts vary across candidate pools

Across five fresh candidate pools, the conservative judge selected a more cautious kept set in all five. Relative separation from the base judge was 0.100 ± 0.092 and changed sign once because the base judge selected an unusually cautious set. K2 uses the observed pool-to-pool distribution as a calibration for the trajectory analysis.

Screen attestation ↗

OFF-TARGET DYNAMICS

Off-target coordinates show different response patterns

The broad battery does not move as one latent “value.” Corrigibility fell in 16/16 essay-training runs, optimism followed content, and risk/agreeableness changed by optimizer state. Every active training cell now carries the same off-target battery.

03 / CURRENT PROGRAM

Current experimental program

The current experiments vary generator state, judge state, and selection rule while holding the remaining loop components fixed. The main outputs are trajectory contrasts, seed-level dispersion, channel coupling, and update geometry.

K1 · QWEN ANCHOR

Judge conditions on a shared Qwen risk organism

One moderate risk organism, four judge arms, four seeds, four rounds, plus a measure-only control.

Primary:evolving self vs frozen base

K2 · OLMo INVERSION

OLMo trajectory contrast under judge inversion

Six-seed conservative-judge vs base-judge contrast, calibrated against the five-pool force distribution.

Primary:baseline-adjusted drift profile

K3 · EM GRID

Neutral-judge trajectories on insecure-code coordinates

Four judge conditions with random-selection control; free generation and self-report are primary because forced choice is floored.

Primary:em_freegen + self-report

CROSS-ORGANISM

Separated judge and generator lineages

A drifted judge and frozen-base judge act on matched fresh generators. Later cells test re-ignition and carrier persistence.

Primary:matched steering-force profiles
